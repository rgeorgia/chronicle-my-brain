Famous Hammers in Myths and History
====================================

1. **Martel**: Charles Martel being a sobriquet in Old French for "The Hammer", was a Frankish political and military leader who, as Duke and Prince of the Franks and Mayor of the Palace, was the de facto ruler of the Franks from 718 until his death.
2. **Mj√∂lnir**: The hammer of Thor, the Norse god of thunder, known for its power and ability to return to Thor after being thrown.
3. **Hephaestus' Hammer**: In Greek mythology, Hephaestus, the god of blacksmiths, crafted weapons and armor for the gods using his hammer.
4. **Excalibur**: While not a hammer, the legendary sword of King Arthur is often associated with the power of divine craftsmanship, similar to that of a blacksmith's hammer.
5. **Vulcan's Hammer**: In Roman mythology, Vulcan was the god of fire and metalworking, often depicted with a hammer in hand, forging weapons for the gods.
6. **The Hammer of the Gods**: A term often used to describe the destructive power of nuclear weapons, symbolizing the ultimate force of human ingenuity and destruction.
7. **The War Hammer**: A medieval weapon designed to crush armor, symbolizing the brutal efficiency of warfare and the evolution of weaponry.
